Fitness Workouts By Age Group

Tailored exercise plans for every stage of life

Workouts for Ages 10-15

At this age, the focus should be on developing fundamental movement skills, building body awareness, and making fitness fun. Exercises should be supervised and appropriate for growing bodies.

Cardiovascular Exercises

Cardio

Jumping Jacks

Start with feet together and arms at sides, then jump to a position with legs apart and arms overhead. 2-3 sets of 20 reps with breaks in between.

Cardio

Obstacle Courses

Set up a fun course with elements that require running, jumping, crawling, and balancing. This improves coordination and makes fitness enjoyable.

Cardio

Dance Sessions

Following dance routines or free dancing to favorite songs for 15-20 minutes improves coordination and cardiovascular health.

Strength Building Exercises

Upper Body

Modified Push-Ups

Perform push-ups from knees rather than toes to build upper body strength gradually. 2 sets of 10 repetitions with proper form.

Core

Superman Holds

Lie face down with arms extended overhead, then lift arms and legs off the ground. Hold for 5-10 seconds, 10 repetitions.

Lower Body

Bodyweight Squats

Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, lower into seated position, then return to standing. Focus on form rather than speed. 3 sets of 12 reps.

Flexibility & Balance

Flexibility

Standing Toe Touches

Stand with feet shoulder-width apart and gently bend at waist to touch toes. Hold for 10-15 seconds, repeat 5 times.

Balance

Flamingo Stand

Stand on one leg with the other foot raised off the ground. Try to maintain balance for 30 seconds, then switch legs.

Safety Notes for This Age Group

  • Focus on proper form rather than weight or intensity
  • Make exercise fun and varied to maintain interest
  • Ensure proper hydration before, during, and after exercise
  • Allow for adequate rest between training sessions
  • Adult supervision is recommended, especially for beginners

Workouts for Ages 16-19

During these years, teens can begin more structured training as their bodies mature. Focus on technique, consistency, and gradual progression rather than heavy weights or extreme intensity.

Cardiovascular Training

Cardio

HIIT Training

High-intensity interval training with 30 seconds of maximum effort followed by 1 minute of recovery. Examples include sprinting, burpees, or jump squats. 15-20 minutes total.

Cardio

Sports Participation

Team sports or individual activities like soccer, basketball, swimming, or cycling provide excellent cardiovascular benefits and teach teamwork.

Cardio

Jogging/Running

Start with intervals of jogging and walking for 20 minutes, gradually increasing jogging time. Aim for 3-4 sessions per week.

Strength Training

Upper Body

Push-Ups

Standard push-ups with hands shoulder-width apart. Focus on full range of motion and controlled movement. 3 sets of 12-15 reps.

Upper Body

Pull-Ups/Assisted Pull-Ups

Begin with assisted pull-ups if needed, working toward unassisted. Focus on engaging back muscles. 3 sets of 8-10 reps.

Lower Body

Lunges

Step forward into a lunge position, lowering until both knees are at 90 degrees. Return and repeat with opposite leg. 3 sets of 10 reps each leg.

Core

Plank Variations

Standard planks, side planks, and plank with leg lifts. Hold each position for 30-60 seconds, 3 sets each.

Flexibility & Mobility

Full Body

Dynamic Stretching Routine

Incorporate leg swings, arm circles, torso rotations, and hip openers before workouts to prepare muscles for activity.

Recovery

Yoga Basics

Basic yoga poses like downward dog, warrior poses, and child's pose to improve flexibility and body awareness. 20-30 minute sessions.

Safety Notes for This Age Group

  • Learn proper form before increasing weight or intensity
  • Allow 48 hours recovery for muscle groups between strength sessions
  • Focus on balanced development rather than aesthetics
  • Stay hydrated and maintain proper nutrition
  • Consider working with a qualified coach or trainer when beginning weight training

Workouts for Ages 20-35

This age range represents peak physical potential for most people. Training can be more intensive and specialized based on individual goals, whether strength, muscle building, endurance, or athletic performance.

Cardiovascular Training

Cardio

Tabata Intervals

20 seconds of maximum effort followed by 10 seconds rest, repeated for 8 rounds (4 minutes). Choose exercises like mountain climbers, burpees, or kettlebell swings.

Cardio

Running Progression

Mix steady-state runs with interval training. Start with 3-5km runs, gradually increasing distance or adding hill training for intensity.

Cardio

Indoor Cycling

Spin classes or programmed cycling workouts with intervals of seated and standing pedaling at varying resistance. 30-45 minute sessions.

Strength Training - Upper Body

Chest

Bench Press

Barbell or dumbbell bench press with appropriate weight. Focus on controlled lowering and powerful pressing. 4 sets of 8-12 reps.

Back

Bent-Over Rows

Using barbell or dumbbells, bend at hips and pull weight toward lower chest/abdomen with elbows close to body. 4 sets of 10-12 reps.

Shoulders

Overhead Press

From standing position, press weights from shoulder level to overhead. Can be done with barbell or dumbbells. 3 sets of 8-10 reps.

Arms

Tricep Dips

Using parallel bars or bench, lower body by bending arms to 90 degrees, then press back up. 3 sets of 12-15 reps.

Strength Training - Lower Body

Legs

Barbell Squats

With barbell across upper back, squat down until thighs are parallel to floor, then return to standing. 4 sets of 8-12 reps.

Legs

Romanian Deadlifts

With slight bend in knees, hinge at hips while keeping back straight to lower weight, then return to standing. 3 sets of 10-12 reps.

Legs

Bulgarian Split Squats

With rear foot elevated on bench, lower into lunge position until front thigh is parallel to floor. 3 sets of 10 reps per leg.

Core

Hanging Leg Raises

Hang from pull-up bar and raise legs to 90 degrees using abdominal strength. Keep movement controlled. 3 sets of 12-15 reps.

Recommended Weekly Training Split

  • Monday: Upper Body Strength + HIIT
  • Tuesday: Lower Body Strength + Core
  • Wednesday: Steady-State Cardio or Active Recovery
  • Thursday: Upper Body Hypertrophy + Core
  • Friday: Lower Body Hypertrophy + HIIT
  • Saturday: Recreational Activity or Sports
  • Sunday: Rest or Light Activity

Workouts for Ages 36-55

During this age range, focus shifts toward maintaining muscle mass, joint health, and metabolic function. Recovery becomes increasingly important, and training should be adapted to address individual limitations.

Cardiovascular Health

Cardio

Zone 2 Training

Moderate-intensity cardio (60-70% of max heart rate) where you can still hold a conversation. Walking, cycling, or swimming for 30-45 minutes, 3-4 times weekly.

Cardio

Rowing Machine

Low-impact full-body cardio. Mix steady-state rowing with intervals. 20-30 minutes, 2-3 times per week.

Cardio

Elliptical Intervals

3 minutes moderate pace followed by 1 minute higher intensity. Repeat for 20-30 minutes total. Joint-friendly cardio option.

Strength Maintenance

Full Body

Dumbbell Thrusters

Holding dumbbells at shoulder height, squat down then drive up and press weights overhead. Combines lower and upper body. 3 sets of 12 reps.

Upper Body

TRX Row

Using suspension trainer, lean back with straight body and pull chest toward handles. Adjustable difficulty based on foot position. 3 sets of 12-15 reps.

Lower Body

Goblet Squats

Hold dumbbell or kettlebell at chest and perform squats with emphasis on depth and form. Helps maintain hip mobility. 3 sets of 12-15 reps.

Core

Bird Dog

From hands and knees, extend opposite arm and leg while maintaining neutral spine. Excellent for core stability. 3 sets of 10 reps each side.

Mobility & Flexibility

Mobility

Hip Mobility Routine

Series of exercises including hip circles, fire hydrants, and seated rotations to maintain hip function. 5-10 minutes daily.

Flexibility

Thoracic Spine Stretches

Foam roller extensions and rotations to maintain upper back mobility and posture. 5 minutes, 3-4 times weekly.

Recovery

Yoga for Adults

Focus on poses that counteract sitting and improve overall mobility. 20-30 minute sessions, 2-3 times weekly.

Important Considerations for This Age Group

  • Prioritize recovery with at least one full rest day between strength sessions
  • Include proper warm-up (5-10 minutes) before any intense activity
  • Consider working with a physical therapist to address any existing limitations
  • Focus on consistent, moderate training rather than extreme sessions
  • Pay attention to joint health through appropriate exercise selection

Workouts for Ages 56+

Exercise becomes increasingly important for maintaining independence, preventing falls, and supporting overall health. Focus on functional movement patterns that support daily activities.

Cardiovascular Health

Cardio

Walking Program

Regular walking with gradual increases in distance or pace. Start with 15-20 minutes and work up to 30+ minutes, 4-5 times per week.

Cardio

Water Aerobics

Joint-friendly cardiovascular exercise in pool setting. Classes typically run 45-60 minutes and provide both cardio and resistance training.

Cardio

Recumbent Bicycle

Seated cycling with back support. Start with 10-15 minutes and gradually increase to 20-30 minutes, 3 times per week.

Functional Strength

Lower Body

Chair Squats

Using chair for safety, practice standing up and sitting down with controlled movement. Progress to holding position just above chair. 2-3 sets of 10-12 reps.

Upper Body

Wall Push-Ups

Standing push-ups against wall, gradually increasing angle of difficulty as strength improves. 2-3 sets of 10-15 reps.

Core

Seated Core Exercises

While seated in chair, perform gentle twists, leg lifts, and forward leans to engage core muscles. 2 sets of 10-12 reps each movement.

Full Body

Resistance Band Work

Using light resistance bands for rows, chest presses, and arm exercises. Focus on control rather than resistance level. 2-3 sets of 12-15 reps.

Balance & Flexibility

Balance

Supported Single-Leg Balance

Practice standing on one leg while holding chair or counter. Progress to unsupported. Hold for 15-30 seconds per leg, 3 times each.

Flexibility

Seated Stretching Routine

Gentle stretches for major muscle groups: neck, shoulders, back, hips, and legs. Hold each stretch 20-30 seconds without bouncing.

Mind-Body

Chair Yoga or Tai Chi

Modified practices focusing on breathing, gentle movement, and balance. 20-30 minute sessions, 2-3 times weekly.

Safety Guidelines for Senior Exercise

  • Consult with healthcare provider before starting new exercise program
  • Always have something stable nearby to hold if needed during balance exercises
  • Stay hydrated and exercise in well-ventilated environments
  • Stop any exercise that causes pain (distinguish between discomfort and pain)
  • Start with shorter sessions (10-15 minutes) and gradually increase duration
  • Consider working with a trainer experienced in senior fitness
